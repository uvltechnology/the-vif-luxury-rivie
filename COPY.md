## Tone & Voice Guidelines

**Overall Tone:**
- Warm but sophisticated
- Confident without being pretentious
- Personal and conversational, not corporate
- Aspirational yet accessible

**Language Patterns:**
- Use sensory details (sun-drenched, golden-hour, azure, lavender)
- Reference specific moments (morning coffee, sunset, long lunches)
- Emphasize experience over amenities
- Balance elegance with ease

**Avoid:**
- Generic travel clichés ("paradise," "dream vacation")
- Overly formal language
- Hard-sell tactics
- Vague descriptions without specifics

**Examples of Good vs. Bad:**
✅ "Wake to the sound of waves, wander to the beach, discover hidden cafés"
❌ "Experience the vacation of a lifetime in paradise"

✅ "Afternoons by the infinity pool, evenings watching the sun paint the sky"
❌ "Enjoy our amazing pool and stunning views"

✅ "We know the best morning bakery nearby, which beach is quietest on weekends"
❌ "We provide excellent local knowledge and customer service"
